Darwin's Theory of Evolution

Two Major Principles that Operate in Darwin's Theory of Evolution:

Natural Selection

Natural selection is the process by which organisms having traits that better enable them to adapt to specific environmental pressures will tend to survive and reproduce in greater numbers than others of their kind, perpetuating these favorable traits.

Sexual Selection

Sexual selection is a form of natural selection that arose through preference by one sex for certain characteristics in individuals of the other sex.

Darwin does not mention the origins of art in any of his works, but he does mention the origins of music. He believed that the origin of music lies in the process of sexual selection.

"No one who admits the agency of natural selection, will dispute that these musical instruments have been acquired through sexual selection" (Darwin 418).
