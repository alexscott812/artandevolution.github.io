Art, Aesthetics, and Evolution

Exploring the relationship between the human species' artistic and aesthetical nature and Darwin's concept of Evolution.

How have species evolved to be artistic and to appreciate art?

"The capacity for art and aesthetics has traditionally been considered one of the features that identify the human species, distinguishing it from its closest living and extinct relatives" (Nadal 169).

Art and artistic appreciation are unique and universal traits of the human species. Such a distinguishing characteristic of a species must have some purpose. How did the artistic nature of the human species originate?
